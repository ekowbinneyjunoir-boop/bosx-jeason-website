STUDENTS’ PROGRESS

Each student will be expected to demonstrate their understanding and ability to explain various information and communication technology terminologies, behavioral and organizational issues, as well as the social and ethical issues associated with information and communication technology concepts and initiatives. The measurement of a student’s mastery of these components will be evaluated both by the quantity and quality of their responses and submissions of assignments. While gaining knowledge of concepts and terminology is a solid foundation for learning, the application of concepts through discussions, cases, and problem solving is essential to develop a meaningful, more substantive knowledge of information and communication technology. Students will be encouraged to contribute significantly to the dialogue of class discussions.

 

ATTENDANCE

It is STRONGLY encouraged that students attend ALL classes. Attendance will be taken at the beginning and end of selected classes. If you cannot attend a class session, please call or e-mail the facilitator in advance.

 

ACADEMIC DISHONESTY

Academic integrity is the pursuit of scholarly activity free from fraud and deception and is an educational objective of this institution. Academic dishonesty includes, but is not limited to, cheating, plagiarizing, fabricating information or citations, facilitating acts of academic dishonesty by others, having unauthorized possession of examination materials, submitting work of another person or work previously used without informing the instructor, or tampering with the academic work of other students.

 To this end, academic dishonesty will not be tolerated. Students who are caught will be disciplined to the full extent according to college procedures.

 

FREEDOM OF EXPRESSION

It is expected that departments and students will respect the views of others when expressed in classroom discussions (LAB).
