REFERENCIES

 

Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs) are transforming the landscape of mental health nursing, offering new tools and approaches that enhance both the quality and accessibility of care. ICTs encompass a broad range of digital technologies designed to capture, store, process, and exchange information electronically, supporting clinical practice in diverse ways. In mental health nursing, these technologies have evolved from simple digital record-keeping to include mobile applications, telehealth platforms, and web-based interventions, all of which facilitate more efficient information management, improved patient monitoring, and innovative models of care delivery.

The integration of ICT in mental health nursing has the potential to revolutionize how nurses interact with patients and manage care. For example, mobile apps and teleconferencing tools enable remote assessment, therapy, and follow-up, breaking down barriers related to distance and accessibility. These technologies not only support clinical decision-making and enhance communication among healthcare providers but also empower patients to engage more actively in their own care. Furthermore, ICTs can help reduce social isolation, streamline administrative tasks, and provide real-time data that informs evidence-based intervention.

Despite these benefits, the full potential of ICT in mental health nursing is yet to be realized, partly due to challenges such as clinician reluctance, technological barriers, and the need for ongoing training and support. Nevertheless, as digital tools become more user-friendly and tailored to the needs of both practitioners and service users, ICTs are poised to play an increasingly central role in promoting mental health, improving service delivery, and supporting the well-being of both patients and nurses.
